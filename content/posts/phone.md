I just bought the same phone I bought two years ago. I did because mine has a battery at 70%, which means I cannot get through a full day on the go. In an office, I'll make it fine. It's the wife that felt I needed to be reachable all day while on holiday. She has a point, I fear. It's not just reachable, I need to be able to pay, navigate, get an uber, show my plane ticket and what have you. I need more than half a day worth of full battery use. 

So why not change the battery? Yeah. I wanted to do that. Turns out, that's a total pain in the rear on an iphone (yes, I know, don't dm me). Having it done was about 100 euros, which is not too bad. Then again... mine's got 128GB of storage. I am at about 85% of that. That's quite close. If I want to use this phone for the next 3 years, which I am, I'll need more storage. Apart from that, bought this phone as a trial. It's my first Apple phone. I bought at the cheap. It was banged up pretty badly. I mean, the screen was superb, just the sides were banged up and the rear had some sort of honey comb pattern from a case lasered into it. No matter how many times  I wiped, it was still shit.

I bought this device for its size more than anything else. Sure, testing IOS against Android would be fun, but really, my wife had this device since it came out, and apart from her case, the size really felt like the goldielocks size to me. No Android phone is at this size anymore.

I loved IOS. I hate to admit it as this is twice Apple "got me". The whole experience blows Android out of the water. All flows, all works and all interacts with other parts of the eco system in such a way that they feel like one. There's no way Android can copy this, as Android does not have its own PC OS. There is no AndroidOS on PC or laptop. I can "Air drop" anything from my phone, to my laptop and vice versa. Heck, I can do so to my wife's electronics too. The experience is just superior.

It's an iPhone 13 mini. It's shipping from Lisbon Portugal. It is in prestine condition and they will have placed a new battery in it, making it hold 100% of a charge when I get it. I got a white one, or "starlight" version this time. The black one shows all marks in plain aluminum, when not rocking a case. I do not rock a case. Anyway, the wife's starlight is like new.

I paid so much for this thing it hurts. This is a phone that's 5 years old and the small version of the "beter" version. Luckily it being Apple, my old phone, despite the relitavely poor condition is still worth one fourth the price of the "new" one, meaning I saved 120 off the price by returning it. So in the end I get a device that will be Apple supported for about 3 years, have a full battery, allow for storing double the gigs I need now and still hit that perfect form factor I am looking for.
